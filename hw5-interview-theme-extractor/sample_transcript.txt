Interviewer: Can you tell me how social media affects the way you see your body?
Participant: I often compare myself with people I see online. Their photos look perfect, and then I start to feel disappointed with my own body. I know those images may be edited, but the comparison still happens automatically.
Interviewer: What helps you deal with those feelings?
Participant: Recently I have tried to practice self-acceptance. I remind myself that my body is not only for appearance. It helps me study, walk, dance, and live my life. That makes me feel more grateful.
Interviewer: Do you think mindfulness is useful?
Participant: Yes, mindfulness helps me notice negative thoughts without immediately believing them. When I feel anxious about my weight or face, I try to pause and breathe. It does not solve everything, but it gives me some distance from the criticism.
Interviewer: What is still difficult?
Participant: The hardest part is comparison. Even when I know I should accept myself, I still compare my body with friends or influencers. It takes time to change that habit.
